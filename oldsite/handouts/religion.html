Religion & Mythology

Subject Guide to Library Resources

Religion is a reverence for a supernatural power that is accompanied by a set of beliefs, values, and rituals or practices based on one's conviction that the supernatural power has a direct affect on the life and destiny of the individual.

Mythology is a collection of (usually) ancient stories belonging to a people which explains their history, gods, origins, and beliefs.

Spirituality is a person's regard for the immaterial, sacred or incorporeal-- the things of the spirit. It is a quality that shows concern and care for the health of one's soul or inner being.

Theology is the study or school of thought about the nature of God and man, practices to be upheld, and other religious topics such as the concept of evil and the afterlife.

Books and articles on the subject of religion are found in the humanities indexes, but as religion is also a subject studied in the fields of sociology, anthropology and other social sciences, that research will also be found in the social science indexes. As you study, you will find that much of the great art and architecture of the world has come from religious expression. On the other hand, you will also find that much of the world's suffering has come in the name of religion from wars, intolerance and prejudice.


St. Jerome, Bible translator, ca. 340-420 A.D. 		A good place to start a paper is to read a little general literature about the topic (encyclopedia, internet article) and look up basic terms that are unfamiliar to you. Dictionaries or encyclopedias can be found in the reference area of the library which specifically deal with the areas of religion or mythology.

The next step would be to look for books which cover or touch upon your specific topic. Search through the chapter headings and indexes of books that seem relevant to zero in on information relating to your specific topic.

Finally, look for journal articles focused on your specific area of inquiry for more in-depth and recent coverage.

When unable to find answers to your questions in these resources, the internet, bibliographies, and reference books will often give you new leads to the answers. Finally, don't forget the wealth of information contained in videos.
