Works Cited Examples:
Chicago Format

There are two documentation systems recommended by The Chicago Manual of Style: the humanities system, which is used by scholars in the fields of literature, history, and the arts, and the author-date system, which is used by scholars in the physical, natural, and social sciences. The examples shown below are first cited according to the humanities system, and then in the author-date system.

Books:

Book with one author

Segal, Nancy L. Entwined Lives: Twins and What They Tell Us about Human Behavior. New York: Dutton, 1991.

Segal, Nancy L. 1991. Entwined lives: Twins and what they tell us about human behavior. New York: Dutton.

Book with two authors

Cohen, Carl, and Tom Regan. The Animal Rights Debate. Lanham, Md.: Rowman & Littlefield Publishers, 2001.

Cohen, Carl, and Tom Regan. 2001. The animal rights debate. Lanham, Md.: Rowman & Littlefield Publishers.

Book with an editor and a later edition

Baird, Robert M., and Stuart E. Rosenbaum, eds. The Ethics of Abortion: Pro-Life vs. Pro-Choice. 3rd ed. Amherst, N.Y.: Prometheus Books, 2001.

Baird, Robert M., and Stuart E. Rosenbaum, eds. 2001. The ethics of abortion: Pro-life vs. pro-choice. 3rd ed. Amherst, N.Y.: Prometheus Books.

Electronic books or e-books:

Book with three or more authors

Hendriks, Jean H., Tony Kaplan, and Dora Black. When Father Kills Mother: Guiding Children through Trauma and Grief. London: Routledge, 2000. http://www.netlibrary.com (accessed June 22, 2006).

Hendriks, Jean H., Tony Kaplan, and Dora Black. 2000. When father kills mother: Guiding children through trauma and grief. London: Routledge. http://www.netlibrary.com (accessed June 22, 2006).

Encyclopedia articles:

Article in a well-known general encyclopedia

Do not list articles from a well-known encyclopedia in the bibliography. Instead, cite the article in notes preceded by s. v. (sub verbo, "under the word") or refer to it in the text, as shown below.

Encyclopedia Americana, 1999, s.v. "dreams."

"In his article on dreams in the 1999 edition of the Encyclopedia Americana, Calvin Hall noted that. . ."

Articles from popular series: Opposing Viewpoints or Taking Sides Books:

Valenti, Jack. "Violence in the Media Should Not Be Blamed for Youth Violence." In Violence: Opposing Viewpoints. Edited by Laura K. Egendorf. San Diego, Calif.: Greenhaven Press, 2001.

Valenti, Jack. 2001. Violence in the media should not be blamed for youth violence. In Violence: Opposing viewpoints. Edited by Laura K. Egendorf. San Diego, Calif.: Greenhaven Press.

Magazine, journal, and newspaper article:

Magazine article

Cowley, Geoffrey. "Girls, Boys and Autism." Newsweek, September 8, 2003, 42-46.

Cowley, Geoffrey. 2003. Girls, boys and autism. Newsweek, September 8, 42-46.

Journal article

Boeckmann, Robert J., and Carolyn Turpin-Petrosino, "Understanding the Harm of Hate Crime." The Journal of Social Issues 58 (2001): 207-225.

Boeckmann, Robert J., and Carolyn Turpin-Petrosino. 2001. Understanding the harm of hate crime. The Journal of Social Issues 58, 207-225.

Newspaper article

In the humanities system, do not cite a news item in the bibliography.

In the author-date system, cite an article from a daily newspaper in the running text, as shown below:
An article in the Los Angeles Times, 19 January 2004, reported that ...

Reports from CQ Researcher:

Weeks, Jennifer. "Nuclear Energy." CQ Researcher 16, no. 10 (March 10, 2006): 217-240.

Weeks, Jennifer. 2006. Nuclear energy. CQ Researcher 16, no. 10 (March 10): 217-240.

Audiovisual:

Videorecording

Jackson, Mick. Tuesdays with Morrie. 89 min. Burbank, Calif.: Touchstone Home Video, 1999. Videocassette.

Jackson, Mick. 1999. Tuesdays with Morrie. 89 min. Burbank, Calif.: Touchstone Home Video. Videocassette.

Magazine article from ProQuest

Terry, W. Scott, and Jennifer S. Burns. 2001. Anxiety and repression in attention and retention. The Journal of General Psychology 128, no. 4 (October): 422-432. http://web5.infotrac.galegroup.com (accessed April 2, 2004).

Lindsley, Chris. 2001. Putting your head in the game: [Final Edition]. The Washington Post, January 26. http://www.proquest.com/ (accessed June 27, 2006).
