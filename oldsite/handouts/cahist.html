To Search for Articles in General on Local History or California History
To search the magazine/newspaper databases listed LBCC Library homepage (Newsbank, ProQuest, SIRS, Facts On File), use words appropriate to your subject, including:
  • Names of local counties or local cities
  • Names of famous local persons
  • Names of local organizations or local institutions
Check in the Library of Congress Subject Headings set (the five big red books on the counter), in dictionaries, thesauri and subject-related books for the right words to use.

SEARCHING FOR BOOKS ON CALIFORNIA HISTORY AND LOCAL HISTORY

LBCC Library catalog is web-based and searchable via the Internet. From the Library homepage, click on the button "Voyager"; the Library Web site's URL is: http://lib.lbcc.edu
Use "Keyword" search or "Subject " search. At the orientation, the librarian will demonstrate on how to do a complex search for more relevant results.

Major "Call Numbers" for United States History and California History:

  • F861 to F870 California History
  • E78 Indians of California
  • E United States History

SEARCHING FOR CALIFORNIA AND LOCAL HISTORY RESOURCES ON THE INTERNET

  • Use a Search Engine such as Altavista (http://altavista.com) when you know the exact terms or names of your topic.
  • Enter two or more related words, each with a +in front of it to make your search more exact:
  • Search for phrases by putting double quotes around two or more words, for example:
    +california +history
    +"mexican immigrants" +california
  • Put an asterisk * after the root word to find plurals or other words beginning with the root.
  • Use subject directories such as Yahoo (http://www.yahoo.com) or Internet indexes such as Librarian's Index to the Internet (http://www.lii.org) for browsing or when you are not sure of the terms or names. Indexes lead you from the general subject to the specific sub-topic.
