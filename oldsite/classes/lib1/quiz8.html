Module 8 - Commercial Databases - Graded assignment

The purpose of this assignment is to give you further hands-on practices with different Web-based databases and their search features. Remember that most databases provide direct e-mail service (i.e., you can e-mail the result of your searches directly to your e-mail account). For this assignment, you may need to do some editing first, then e-mail the results to me either as a message or as an attachment to your e-mail message.

Instruction
Go to the library Web site at http://lib.lbcc.cc.ca.us, you will see a list of database names under the Databases heading. Select each database by clicking on the link name. from LBCC Library's homepage and log-in using the user ID and password given you at the beginning of the course. Read each question carefully and give the answer according to the requests in the question.

A. ProQuest Direct
1. Publication Search
Search for a magazine called American Economist. Select the latest issue (the latest issue is always listed first, keep in mind that sometimes the latest issue carries a future date of publication, even before the print copy hits the newsstand).

  • Give the exact term (s) you typed in for the search.
  • Does this periodical offer full-text articles?

2. Advanced Search
Advanced Search allows you to search simultaneously up to three different fields, plus other limiting features to help you customize your search. Advanced Search is available only in the Word Search mode; select Advanced Search by clicking on the tab Advanced, type in your search terms and select the corresponding field for the search terms. Refer to ProQuest Direct online help, if need be.
Perform an advanced search on the topic of overcrowded schools in the Los Angeles Times newspaper.

  • Give the exact term (s) you typed in for the search and the corresponding fields you selected for the terms.
  • Give one relevant citation.

B. NewsBank
NewsBank is comprised of four different databases; to select a database, click on the database's name link.
1. Select Business NewsBank. Do a search on the topic of the Exxon Mobil merger and find out the approximate dollar amount of the deal.
2. Search Global NewsBank and find out what is the main issue concerning the squatters in Zimbabwe.
3. Search NewsBank NewsFile Collection to find out who is Julia "Butterfly" Hill and what was her crusade about?
4. Search StatBank to answer the two following questions:

a. What is Saudi Arabia's total export of refined petroleum products in 1997? (Note: The amount is given in barrels)
Hint: StatBank is a structured database, starting from the broadest subject to the specific subdivisions of the subject).

b. Describe your search path down the subject tree. (i.e. how many levels or subdivisions you have to click to find the needed information. Give the names of the subdivisions.

C. Infotrac - Health Reference Center
Perform a search on the topic of "high protein diet" in professional publications only. You can limit this search by checking the appropriate box under the option Limit the current search. Look at the search screen carefully to find the correct checkmark box. Answer the following questions:


a. How many hits did you obtain?
b. Are there full-text articles available?
c. List one full-text article citation.

D. Books-in-Print - Search this database and answer the following questions:


a. Give the price and the publication date of the book entitled Stiffed by Susan Faludi.
b. What is the subject of this book?

E. CollegeSource Online - Search this database and answer the following questions:


a. How many colleges are there within 10-miles radius of Long Beach?
b. Give the name of the college located in Wilmington, California.

F. MediaNet - Search MediaNet to find out if Long Beach City College Library owns videos relating to the subject of obsessive compulsive disorder.


a. How many videos does LBCC Library own?
b. List the title of the most recent video on this subject.
