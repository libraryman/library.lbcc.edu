COURSE DESCRIPTION

This one-unit course teaches basic college-level research skills necessary for effective use of 
traditional and contemporary information resources. Emphasis is placed on the ability to develop 
search strategies and evaluate information sources. We will cover different levels and types of 
information in both print and electronic formats, including information available over the Internet.

LEARNING OUTCOMES

At the completion of this course, you will be able to do the following:
	
	* Describe the physical organization of a library, different types of library services, and library resources.
	
	* Demonstrate an understanding of classification systems, Library of Congress subject 
	  headings, and the concept of a controlled vocabulary.
	  
	* Search the library's online catalog and interpret a bibliographic record.
	
	* Demonstrate knowledge of discipline-specific reference sources and how to use them.
	
	* Demonstrate an ability to use indexes of various types and formats.
	
	* Differentiate between various Internet protocols and tools.
	
	* Document sources adhering to MLA (Modern Language Association) style guidelines.
	
	* Evaluate information sources according to specific criteria.
	
	* Apply critical-thinking skills to construct effective research methods, including 
	  defining a topic, mapping a search strategy, and using appropriate resources.

COURSE ORGANIZATION

The course is divided into nine units. The units are as follows:

	1. Introduction to the Research Process
	
	2. Navigating the Library and Evaluating Sources
	
	3. Finding Books and E-Books
	
	4. Reference Sources
	
	5. Periodicals and Using ProQuest
	
	6. Online Research Databases
	
	7. Copyright and Citing Sources
	
	8. Creating a Research Guide
	
	9. The Internet and the Future of Information
