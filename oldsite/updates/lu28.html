Library Orientations Enhance
Student Achievement


Good things happen when LBCC faculty bring their classes to the library to meet with a librarian. Students discover how to make the best use of the library's information resources, both its physical collection and its "virtual" one-the extensive array of electronic databases that the library subscribes to and that can be accessed "anytime/anywhere."

For Spring, 2005 the scheduling program introduced late last fall continues; it's designed to reach more students by being more convenient for instructional faculty. Here are the guidelines:

  • Schedule requested dates two or more weeks in advance
  • Librarians are available to meet with classes at LAC:
    • between 7:15 a.m. and 9 p.m. Monday through Thursday
    • between 7:15 a.m. and 3 p.m. on Friday
    • between 10:15 a.m. and 3 p.m. on Saturday

Check with PCC Library (x3028) for orientation hours at that site.

  • Classes normally will meet in L 208 (Open Access Lab, 2nd floor)
  • Requests for an orientation may be made by phone (x 4853 for LAC, x 3028 for PCC) or using the online form available at the library home page (http://lib.lbcc.edu/) click on "Services for Faculty."

For more information contact: Kim Barclay, kbarclay@lbcc.edu
