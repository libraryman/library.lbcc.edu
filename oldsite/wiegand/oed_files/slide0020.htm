Special Characters used in the OED
nPronunciations are shown in the standard characters of the International Phonetic Alphabet.
nGreek characters are displayed but are not searchable.
nAccented letters If you want to use a Find Word or Full text search to look for a word with an accented letter (e.g. café) simply type a plain letter in place of the accented one (e.g. cafe).
nHyphens In a Find Word search ignores hyphens and does not distinguish hyphens and spaces.  This means that typing database will find database, data-base, or data base.
nIn a Full Text search, however, typing data-base will find both data-base and data base, but not find database you have to type it in exactly, I.e. as one word.
nCase In a Find Word or Full Text search you can use upper or lower case letter to type your search term.  For example, “dickens” will find “Dickens” and “dickens.”
