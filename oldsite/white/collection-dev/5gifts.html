Collection Development Policy Statement

Gift Material

Gifts, both individual items and a collection, may be valuable additions to the collections. Donors are asked to sign a Gift Material Release form at the time the gift is made. The Collection Development Librarian is consulted to review gift materials, and no conditions as to retention or treatment are accepted. Gift materials are evaluated by the same criteria as those for new material purchases. The Library acknowledges receipt of gifts but does not place a monetary value on the materials. The Collection Development Librarian determines whether donated materials meet the collection-development criteria. Donors agree that the items donated become the property of the Library to be disposed of, as the Library sees fit, including but not limited to, allocation to library collections, to book sales, to other individuals or organizations, or to deselection.

Because of the inherent processing and storage costs entailed in the incorporation of gifts into the holdings, the Library reserves the right to prescreen a collection when it is offered, to select titles that fall within the areas collected, and to decline materials outside the scope of the collection-development policy. Gifts of outdated textbooks, titles already in the collection, materials in poor condition, and backruns of most journals are therefore discouraged.

The Library does not accept gifts of unauthorized copies of sound or video recordings.

Gifts with a value greater than $100 cannot be handled directly by the School of Learning Resources, Teaching, & Technologies but must be presented through the Board of Trustees.
