Search Engines

A search engine, also known as a “spider,” “crawler,” or “robot,” is a software program that continuously explores the World Wide Web, assembling information about Web documents, then storing that information in a huge database.

This database is made available to the public for searching. Search engines are particularly useful when you have a specific topic in mind, or when you know the terms, keywords, or names to use in your search.
