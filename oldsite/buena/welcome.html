On behalf of the Library faculty and staff, I welcome you to Long Beach City College Library, comprising the Liberal Arts Campus (LAC) Library and the Pacific Coast Campus (PCC) Library. The Library is a department within the School of Learning Resources, Teaching, and Technologies.


The Information Age has ushered in an extraordinary array of digital technologies along with attendant challenges. The ability to retrieve relevant information fast and efficiently, whether for academic purposes, personal need, or lifelong learning, demands the critical apparatus of discerning consumers of information. In striving to provide the College community with the latest information technology and value-added service, the Library is deeply committed to the College's educational mission and instructional goals. In recent years, the Library faculty has particularly focused on the goal of working collaboratively with the faculty from other disciplines to integrate information literacy across the curriculum.

With the advent of  Voyager, the Library's Web-based catalog, subscriptions to numerous online databases, and electronic books, the Library transcends the physical confines of its brick-and-mortar structure to provide information around the clock, "anytime, anywhere." By entering into resource-sharing partnerships and reciprocal agreements with other libraries, the Library has further extended information access to on-campus users and our growing online community of distance learners. In the near future, we hope to offer virtual reference service, available 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

In pursuit of the goal to prepare students to function effectively in an ever-changing environment, a technological society, and an information-based economy, the Library faculty is sensitive to diverse learning styles and delivers customized instruction in workshops, orientations, face-to-face courses, Web-enhanced courses, and online courses.

The Library faculty and staff, reflecting Southern California's richly diverse population, draw on their ethnic and cultural roots to bring an acute awareness of the variety of values and perspectives represented in those whom we serve. Contributing to a cosmopolitan worldview, some of the faculty and staff members hail from other countries, such as Vietnam, Nigeria, the Philippines, Mexico, and India.

Please consider LBCC Library as your partner in learning. We value and appreciate your input--no matter how small--in helping us to improve our service to you. Towards this end, I encourage you to share with me your suggestions concerning the Library's services and resources and would like you to know that my door is open for useful and productive discussions.

Nenita B. Buenaventura
