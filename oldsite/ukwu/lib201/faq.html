Frequently Asked Questions

I am experiencing technical difficulties. I can't access the course Web site, or I can't send e-mail, or I can't attach a copy of my assignment to an e-mail message. What should I do?

Whenvever you experience problems, such as those above, call the Long Beach City College Distance Learning HelpLine. The HelpLine is at (562) 938-4818, and it is open Monday through Thursday, from 8 am to 9 pm, and on Friday, from 8 am to 4 pm. If the line is busy, then the voicemail system will be activated. However, please DO NOT leave a message on the voicemail; HANG UP and call again a few minutes later. The HelpLine will not be able to return your calls.

Can I access online databases and materials when I'm not on campus?

Current Long Beach City College students, faculty, and staff have off-campus access to library databases and online materials. A password is not needed to access Voyager, the Library's online catalog; however, passwords are needed to access the electronic books and the commercial databases, such as ProQuest.

How are my grades determined?

Grading is based upon the completion of the assignments and bibliography. Each week, you will be asked to e-mail a completed assignment(s) to your instructor, and a bibliography on the final week. You can earn up to 11 points for the eight graded assignments, and 12 points for the bibliography, for a total of 100 points. Here is the point breakdown:

  • A   90-100 points
  • B   80-89 points
  • C   70-79 points
  • D   60-69 points
  • F   below 60 points

In order to earn a grade of "CR," students must accumulate at least 70 points.

How can I check on my grades?

You may check your grades at any time by clicking on the button labeled "Grades" on the left side of your screen. Then, enter the section number for the course and your seven-digit student number. You will be able to see all the grades you have earned so far in this class. You will not, however, be able to see the grades of other students, nor can they see yours.

If I'm having difficulty understanding the material, can I make an appointment to see my instructor?

Yes, if you need help, you may e-mail Instructor Monica White at mwhite@lbcc.edu, or call her on the phone at (562) 938-4582. She will make an appointment to work with you on an individual, face-to-face basis at the Liberal Arts Campus Library.

My computer is not working. Is there anyplace I can go to use a computer so that I can access the course Web site?

Yes, you can use the computers in the Research Centers and Labs. at either the Liberal Arts Campus Library or the Pacific Coat Campus Library. Just plan on using them during library hours, as posted on the Library's homepage at lib.lbcc.edu/libhours.html.
