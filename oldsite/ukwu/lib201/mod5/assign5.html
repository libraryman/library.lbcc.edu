Assignment 5: Periodical Indexes in Print Format

The purpose of this assignment is to acquire skill in interpreting citations, identifying main subject headings in print periodical indexes, and selecting appropriate indexes for specific topics. Although some topics are covered in different indexes, please select the index that most closely matches the topic in question.

If one of the resources you need is not on the shelf where it belongs, and you cannot find it, just write "NOS" as your answer. NOS stands for "not on shelf."

Instructions

You are asked to find periodical articles on six topics, written between 2003-2004. You will only use H.W. Wilson print periodical indexes, which are published annually in a bound volume with the year covered marked on the spine (e.g. the Humanities Index annually bound volume is from April of the previous year to March of the following year: April 2003-March 2004). There are also supplements published quarterly in paperback volumes. Search LBCC's library catalog or any library catalog near your home for the call number and the location of the indexes. The six periodical indexes to be used for this assignment are:

  1. Art Index - November 2003-October 2004
  2. Business Periodicals Index - August 2003-July 2004
  3. Education Index - July 2003 - June 2004
  4. Humanities Index - April 2003- March 2004
  5. Social Sciences Index - April 2003 - March 2004
  6. General Science Index - June 2003 - May 2004

For this assignment, use the bound volumes listed above. Use one different index for each topic. Answer all questions related to each topic. List all citations in MLA style.

  1. Topic: Find periodical articles on the subject of chlorofluorocarbons.
    1. List the title of the index you chose for this topic.
    2. List the main subject heading and the subdivision (if any), under which the articles are found. (Note: main headings are in bold face type; subdivisions are also in bold face type and centered.)
    3. List one of the related topics for this main subject heading. (Note: related topics are listed under See also.)
    4. List the citation of the third article.
  2. Topic: Find periodical articles on the subject of television in education.
    1. List the title of the index you chose for this topic.
    2. List the main subject heading and the first two subdivisions under which the articles are found. (Note: main headings are in bold face type; subdivisions are also in bold face type and centered.)
    3. List one of the related topics for this main subject heading. (Note: related topics are listed under See also.)
    4. List the title and the date of the periodical for the first article.
    5. Does the second article have a bibliography? Illustrations?
  3. Topic: Find periodical articles on the subject of magic realism (literature).
    1. List the title of the index you chose for this topic.
    2. List the main subject heading and the subdivision (if any), under which the articles are found. (Note: main headings are in bold face type; subdivisions are also in bold face type and centered.)
    3. List one of the related topics for this main subject heading. (Note: related topics are listed under See also.)
    4. List the title of an article about The Mistress of Spices.
  4. Topic: Find periodical articles on the subject of insider trading.
    1. List the title of the index you chose for this topic.
    2. List the main subject heading and the subdivision (if any), under which the articles are found. (Note: main headings are in bold face type; subdivisions are also in bold face type and centered.)
    3. List one of the related topics for this main subject heading. (Note: related topics are listed under See also.)
    4. Look for the article entited "Scandal Cheat Sheet." What type of article is it? (Hint: the answer is found between brackets.)
  5. Topic: Find periodical articles on the subject of digital photography.
    1. List the title of the index you chose for this topic.
    2. List the main subject heading and the first three subdivisions under which the articles are found. (Note: main headings are in bold face type; subdivisions are also in bold face type and centered.)
    3. List one of the related topics for this main subject heading. (Note: related topics are listed under See also.)
    4. List the title and the author of the article from Communication Arts Magazine, March/April 2003.
    5. Does this article have illustrations?
  6. Topic: Find periodical articles on the subject of conflict management.
    1. List the title of the index you chose for this topic.
    2. List the main subject heading and the subdivision (if any), under which the articles are found. (Note: main headings are in bold face type; subdivisions are also in bold face type and centered.)
    3. List one of the related topics for this main subject heading. (Note: related topics are listed under See also.)
    4. Write down the citation for the first article.
    5. Does the first article have a bibliography and a table?
