Course Description

This one-unit course teaches basic college-level research skills necessary for effective use of traditional and contemporary information resources. Emphasis is placed on the ability to develop research strategies and evaluate information resources. We will cover the different levels, types, and formats of information, including the Internet.

Course Objectives

At the completion of this course, you will be able to do the following:

  • Describe the physical organization of a library, different types of library services, and library resources
  • Demonstrate an understanding of classification systems, Library of Congress subject headings, and the concept of a controlled vocabulary.
  • Search the library's online catalog and interpret a bibliographic record.
  • Demonstrate knowledge of discipline-specific reference sources and how to use them.
  • Demonstrate an ability to use indexes of various types and formats.
  • Differentiate various Internet protocols and tools.
  • Document sources adhering to MLA (Modern Language Association) style guidelines.
  • Evaluate information sources according to specific criteria.
  • Apply critical-thinking skills to construct effective research methods, including defining a topic, mapping a search strategy, and using appropriate resources.

Course Organization

The course is divided into nine modules. Each module corresponds to one week (two classroom hours) in the college semester. The modules are, as follows:

  1. Library arrangement and online catalog
  2. Classification systems and Library of Congress subject headings
  3. Introduction to the research process
  4. Information evaluation and citation of sources
  5. Indexes
  6. Reference resources
  7. Electronic databases
  8. Introduction to the Internet
  9. Review. Compilation of a bibliography

We will use the Internet and will visit various web sites throughout the course. The links were thoroughly checked at the beginning of the course; however, due to the volatility of the Web environment, please inform the instructor of non-valid links.

Grading

Bibliography: 12 points

Assignments: 11 points for each of the eight assignments for a total of 88 points.

Maximum points: 100

Note: Students' work will be graded based upon the quality of content and the aesthetic/orderly appeal of the presentation. Points will be deducted for careless/sloppy work.

Here is the breakdown of points:

  • A   90 points and above
  • B   80-89 points
  • C   70-79 points
  • D   60-69 points
  • F   below 60 points

Students have the option to choose between a credit/no credit or a letter grade for the course. With the credit/no credit option, students must complete and submit all eight graded assignments and the bibliography on or before the end of the course and accumulate a total of at least 70 points.

Since this is a credit/no credit course, students who wish to receive a letter grade must go to the Admissions Office and fill out the necessary form before 30% of the course is completed. Note that once a letter grade is elected by the student and is awarded, it cannot be reversed at a later time. To receive credit for the course, students must submit ALL 8 required written assignments, plus the bibliography, by the end of the course.
