Assignment 8: Internet Resources

The purpose of this assignment is to gain practice doing research on the Internet, using subject directories and search engines, and to acquire the skills needed to evaluate Internet resources.

Instructions

Take time to read the questions and the instructions within each question and submit your answer accordingly. To perform a keyword search, type in the search term (s) in the search box and press Enter or click on Search. The search box is usually located next to the Search button. Search buttons may be labeled differently with such names as Find, or Submit, or Go Get It, depending on the website searched.

Subject Directories

Academic Subject Directories

  1. Go to Infomine at http://infomine.ucr.edu and search for information on hygrogen energy. Do not search by keyword, but follow the subject tree path to find a list of resources related to hydrogen energy.
    1. Describe the path you followed (i.e. how many levels or clicks did you have to go to in order to find results).
    2. List one relevant website, including its URL and title.
  2. Go to the Librarians' Internet Index at http://www.lii.org and enter a keyword search for information on alternative sources of energy.
    1. List the keyword(s) you used for this search and any modification of your search terms.
    2. List one relevant website and include its URL and title.
    3. Evaluate the annotations for this website in terms of clarity and usefulness.

Commercial Subject Directory

  1. Go to the Yahoo! Subject Directory (towards the bottom of the page) at http://www.yahoo.com and search for information on computer graphics—specifically, animation. You will perform two search modes, as follow:
    1. Browse search
      Follow the subject tree path starting with the broadest main heading, then click down to the subdivisions to get to "animation." Yahoo! makes it easy to follow the thread: at each subdivision page, you will see at the top of the page your step-by-step path down the subject tree. List all of the subdivision names that you have clicked to get to "animation."
    2. Keyword search
      Write down the exact search terms you typed in the search box and any modification of your search terms. Compare the two search modes in terms of speed and ease of use.

Search Engines

Single Search Engines

  1. Enter the following search in the two search engines listed below: Find a short biography about Dolores Huerta.
    Google at http://www.google.com
    Ask.com at http://ask.com
    1. Write down the exact search terms you typed in the search box and any modification of your search terms.
    2. How many of the first 10 retrieved websites would you consider relevant? Why?
    3. How far did you have to scroll down to find a relevant Web page?
    4. List a relevant website where Huerta's biography is found. Include the URL and the title of the website.
    5. What is Dolores Huerta' date of birth?
    6. What is the name of the labor organization she founded?
  2. Enter the following search in MSN at http://www.msn.com
    Perform a search to find information about stem cell research and George Bush.
    1. Write down the exact search terms you typed in the search box and any modification of your search terms.
    2. List a relevant website where the information may be found. Include the URL and the title of the website.
    3. When was this website last modified?
  3. Enter the same search as above in Yahoo! Search at http://search.yahoo.com
    1. Write down the exact search terms you typed in the search box and any modification of your search terms.
    2. List a relevant website where the information may be found. Include the URL and the title of the website.
    3. When was this website last modified?

Meta Search Engine

  1. Go to Vivisimo's Clusty at http://clusty.com and search for resources related to the hybrid cars.
    1. Write down the exact search terms you typed in the search box and any modification of your search terms.
    2. Briefly scan through the first page of the results list. On the average, how many search engines are listed with each result entry? (Hint: Search engines are listed on the last line of the entry.)
    3. Which search engine appears the most often in the entries?
    4. List one relevant website including its URL and its title, and the search engines that found this website.

Evaluation of Websites

  1. Visit and evaluate the three websites listed below. Then, answer the following questions for all three websites.
    • http://www.cis.org
    • http://www.minutemanproject.com
    • http://uscis.gov/graphics/index.htm
    1. What is the title of the website?
    2. Who are the website's sponsors, and is there any information given about the sponsors? (Hint: Information about the sponsors is usually found by clicking on the link titled "About us." Is there such a link on this website?)
    3. Is this website educational, informational, or propagandadistic?
    4. Would you consider this website reliable, and would you use it for your research? Why?

Save your file as a .doc document, and send it as an attachment to an e-mail to your instructor, or type the answer as an e-mail message, and send it to your instructor.
