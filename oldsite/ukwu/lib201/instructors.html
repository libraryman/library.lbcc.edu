Hi. As one of your instructors, I look forward to assisting you through this course.

As long as I can remember, I've always wanted to be a librarian. Attracted to the world of books, libraries, and research, I've worked in various environments. Back in the late 1970s, I worked for a dealer who bought rare books and manuscripts and sold them to museums, libraries, and wealthy private collectors around the world. After that, I spent eight years as an information researcher in the research library at Northrop Corporation and gained experience doing legal research for the attorneys. For the past 17 years, I've been a librarian at Long Beach City College and have held various positions, including department chairperson. Working at Long Beach City College Library is, by far, my most enjoyable assignment.

I have a Bachelors Degree in Applied Linguistics, with a minor in French, from Georgetown University in Washington, D.C. I earned my Masters Degree in Library Science at the University of Southern California. In that program, law librarianship was one of my favorite courses. Not surprisingly, I love to read, especially literature, and in 2001, I earned a Masters Degree in English at California State University, Long Beach, specializing in British literature of the Restoration and Eighteenth Century. In recent years, I've studied Web technologies, Web development, and graphic design.

In recent years, I've studied Web technologies, Web development, and graphic design and earned a certificate in Web technologies from the International Webmasters Association.
