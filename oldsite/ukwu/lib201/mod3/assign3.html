Assignment 3: The Research Process

For this assignment, you will submit a topic statement and an overview of your search strategy. You will need to write a clear description in a complete sentence of the topic you choose. Use complete sentences and delineate your paper's point of view. The topic you select will be used in future assignments, specifically the compilation of a bibliography in the final week. For assistance with this assignment, please refer to the sample search model in Study Guide 3 or the following guidelines.

  1. Decide on a current controversial topic. To find potential topics, you may consult print reference resources, such as CQ Researcher, Issues and Controversies on File, or SIRS (Social Issues Resources Series, not available at LBCC Library). The indexes in these works list topics alphabetically.

    You may also find inspiration for a research topic by accessing electronic databases. For example, you might consult Voyager, LBCC Library's online catalog, or databases, such as ProQuest or Opposing Viewpoints, for subject headings and subheadings to help you narrow your topic.

    The Library of Congress Subject Headings lists subject headings along with their narrower, broader, and related topics.

    To find synonyms, consult a thesaurus. A thesaurus may be a book or an online database. A thesaurus lists words and their synonyms. Online thesauri may be found at

    http://www.m-w.com/home.htm
    http://www.thesaurus.com
  2. Write your search statement in a complete sentence including a point of view on the topic.
  3. Identify major concepts of your topic and write them down.
  4. Think of synonyms for these concepts and use them, as necessary.
  5. Describe which resource(s) (books, periodicals, Internet, interviews, etc.) you would use for a short one-page essay on your topic, and give detailed reasons why they are appropriate for this assignment.
